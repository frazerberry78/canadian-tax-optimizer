# 2026 Canadian Tax Rules

## Income Tax Rates
For the 2026 tax year, the federal income tax rates are as follows:

- **15%** on the first $53,359 of taxable income
- **20.5%** on the next $53,359 (from $53,359 to $106,717)
- **26%** on the next $58,750 (from $106,717 to $165,468)
- **29%** on the next $64,533 (from $165,468 to $230,001)
- **33%** on income over $230,001

## Provincial Tax Rates
Each province has its own tax rates:

### Ontario

- **5.05%** on the first $47,630
- **9.15%** on the next $11,420 (from $47,630 to $59,050)
- **11.16%** on the next $11,240 (from $59,050 to $70,290)
- **12.16%** on the next $11,930 (from $70,290 to $82,220)
- **13.16%** on income over $82,220

### British Columbia

- **5.06%** on the first $42,184
- **7.7%** on the next $11,874 (from $42,184 to $54,058)
- **10.5%** on the next $11,614 (from $54,058 to $65,672)
- **12.29%** on the next $21,643 (from $65,672 to $87,315)
- **14.7%** on income over $87,315

## Tax Credits
Some available tax credits include:

- Basic personal amount: $14,398
- Spousal amount: $14,398
- Canada Employment Amount: $1,287
- Disability Tax Credit: $8,662

## Deductions
Tax deductions include:

- RRSP Contributions
- Childcare Expenses
- Interest on Student Loans

## Tax Filing Deadline
The deadline for filing individual tax returns is April 30, 2027. If you owe taxes and file after this date, you may incur penalties.

## Special Considerations
- **First-Time Home Buyers' Tax Credit**: Eligible individuals can claim a non-refundable tax credit of up to $8,000.
- **Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA)**: Contribution limit of $6,500.
